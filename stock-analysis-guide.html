What is Stock Analysis?

Stock analysis is the systematic evaluation of a company's financial health, business prospects, and stock price to determine its investment potential. Professional investors use two primary approaches:

📊 Fundamental Analysis

Examines company financials, earnings, revenue growth, debt levels, and competitive position to determine intrinsic value.

📈 Technical Analysis

Studies price charts, trading volume, and market patterns to predict future price movements and optimal entry/exit points.

Essential Stock Analysis Metrics

Valuation Ratios

  • P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings): Compare stock price to earnings per share
  • P/B Ratio (Price-to-Book): Stock price relative to book value per share
  • PEG Ratio: P/E ratio adjusted for earnings growth rate
  • Price-to-Sales: Market cap divided by annual revenue

Financial Health Indicators

  • Debt-to-Equity Ratio: Company's financial leverage
  • Current Ratio: Ability to pay short-term obligations
  • Return on Equity (ROE): Profitability relative to shareholder equity
  • Free Cash Flow: Cash generated after capital expenditures

Growth Metrics

  • Revenue Growth: Year-over-year sales increase
  • Earnings Growth: EPS growth rate over time
  • Dividend Growth: Historical dividend increase pattern

Technical Analysis Essentials

Key Technical Indicators

Trend Indicators

  • Moving Averages (50-day, 200-day)
  • MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)
  • Bollinger Bands

Momentum Indicators

  • RSI (Relative Strength Index)
  • Stochastic Oscillator
  • Volume Analysis

Chart Patterns

Learn to identify key patterns like head and shoulders, double tops/bottoms, triangles, and support/resistance levels that signal potential price movements.

Step-by-Step Analysis Process

1

Company Overview

Research the company's business model, industry position, competitive advantages, and recent news.

2

Financial Analysis

Examine income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow statements for the past 3-5 years.

3

Valuation Assessment

Calculate key ratios and compare to industry peers and historical averages.

4

Technical Analysis

Analyze price charts, identify trends, and determine optimal entry/exit points.

5

Risk Assessment

Evaluate potential risks including market, industry, and company-specific factors.
